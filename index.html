Tribute to Eazy-E

Eric Lynn Wright - "Eazy-E"

The man who brought West Coast hip hop to the forefront of American music.

 

Background info on NWA

Eazy-E's relationship with Dr. Dre and his other band mates in N.W.A. became strained and eventually fell apart, leading to solo careers for each member and the end of the group.

Timeline of Eazy's Life

 

    1964: Eric Lynn Wright, also known as Eazy-E, is born in Compton, California.
    1983: Eazy-E co-founded the rap group N.W.A along with Dr. Dre, Ice Cube, MC Ren, and DJ Yella.
    1985: Eazy-E dropped out of high school, but went on to become a successful rapper and business man.
    1988: N.W.A. released their debut album, "Straight Outta Compton", which was a commercial and critical success and helped popularize gangsta rap.
    1991: Eazy-E released his solo debut album, "Eazy-Duz-It", which was also well-received and cemented his reputation as a solo artist.
    1992: Eazy-E founded his own record label, Ruthless Records, which he used to release albums by N.W.A. and other artists.
    1994: Eazy-E had several negative interactions with the police, including arrests for drug possession and assault.
    1995: Eazy-E was almost killed in a drive-by shooting, but survived the incident.
    1995: Eazy-E was diagnosed with AIDS, and passed away on March 26 of the same year at the age of 31.
